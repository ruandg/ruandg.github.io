About Me

Ruan D. Gomes is a computer scientist and has a D.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering from the Universidade Federal de Campina Grande (UFCG). He is a professor at the Instituto Federal de Educação, Ciência e Tecnologia da Paraíba (IFPB) since 2012. Currently he is a professor of the Informatics Coordination at Campus Campina Grande, and gives classes in the areas of programming fundamentals, design and analysis of algorithms, wireless networks, distributed systems, and embedded systems for the undergraduate courses of Telematics and Computer Engineering, as well as for the technician courses on informatics. He is also a permanent member of the Post-Graduate Program in Information Technology of IFPB. He has experience in R&D projects, and worked as principal investigator or researcher in projects funded by companies such as Petrobras, Eletrobras, and RNP, as well as by government funding agencies, such as CNPq. His main areas of research and development are wireless networks, embedded systems, and multimedia distributed systems.

Research Projects

Ongoing Projects

Project Title: Universal CNPq - Development of New Mechanisms and Protocols to Increase the Reliability of Industrial Wireless Sensor Networks
Function: Principal Investigator
Duration: 36 months (February 2019 - January 2022)
Quantity: R$ 23.818,95
Purpose: The general goal of this project is to propose and evaluate new protocols and mechanisms, such as link quality estimators and adaptive channel diversity mechanisms, to increase reliability of Industrial Wireless Sensor Networks.
Project Title: Interconecta IFPB - Dynamic Modulation Selection in IEEE 802.15.4g Networks
Function: Principal Investigator
Duration: 9 months (April 2019 - December 2020)
Quantity: R$ 9.600,00
Purpose: This project has as goal proposing and evaluating new mechanisms for dynamic modulation selection, based on the physical layer configurations defined on the IEEE 802.15.4g standard.
